## Design Context

### Users
Greek shoppers — primarily mobile-first users (Android phones) who compare grocery prices across major supermarkets (Lidl, AB Βασιλόπουλος, Σκλαβενίτης, MyMarket, Μασούτης, Γαλαξίας, etc.). They open the app while shopping in-store, at home planning meals, or checking weekly deals. They are practical and value-driven — they want information fast, without visual noise.

### Brand Personality
**Practical · Calm · Trustworthy**

The app is a daily utility — not a lifestyle brand. It should feel like a well-designed tool: dependable, quick to scan, and unobtrusive. Think of a well-organized market shelf: clear labels, clean space, easy to navigate. Not flashy, not corporate.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Both light and dark mode. Dark is preferred at night / dim environments; light for bright outdoor use.
- **Visual tone**: Minimal and refined. Surfaces should breathe. Colors should be muted and purposeful — no intense purples or neon gradients.
- **Light mode**: Soft warm-whites and cool-slate neutrals. Accents in slate-indigo (muted, not electric). Background: near-white with a subtle warm tint.
- **Dark mode**: Deep near-black with slight blue tint (not OLED black). Muted indigo accents, not glowing. Text at 90% white, not pure white.
- **Typography**: Clean, readable, slightly condensed for product names. Hierarchy through weight + size contrast, not color.
- **Motion**: Minimal and purposeful. Entrance animations only. No continuous loops except clock.
- **No gradients on text**: flat solid colors only.
- **No heavy glassmorphism**: subtle backdrop blur is acceptable for floating elements, not for cards.

### Design Principles
1. **Speed first**: Every visual decision should make the app feel faster — skeleton states, instant feedback, no heavy paint.
2. **Information density over decoration**: Product cards need name, price, and market icon — everything else is secondary.
3. **Calm color restraint**: Use color only to communicate state (sale = green/red, premium = soft gold). Not for decoration.
4. **Thumb-friendly**: All interactive targets ≥ 44px. Primary actions at the bottom within thumb reach.
5. **Offline-graceful**: The UI should always feel functional even when the backend is syncing — cached data, clear status indicators.
